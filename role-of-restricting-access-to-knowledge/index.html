Role of restricting access to knowledge

Typically, only a fully initiated elder would know and understand the entire knowledge system of the community. By keeping critical information sacred and restricted, the so-called ‘Chinese whispers effect’ could be avoided, protecting information from corruption. Source

  • Is it an ethical stand? Shouldn't knowledge be free and available to all?
  • Is it only relevant to oral traditions?

The wisdom of elders comes from the ability to see the system from a higher ground. Where everybody is blinded by chaos, they can see the pattern. More than merely acquiring knowledge, one should detach and reflect on it.
